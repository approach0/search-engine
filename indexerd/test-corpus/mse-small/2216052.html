You know that all solutions to your PDE satisfying the left and right boundary conditions are of the form
[imath]u(x,y)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty (a_n \cosh(n \pi y/a) + b_n \sinh(n \pi y/a)) \sin(n \pi x/a)[/imath]
for some [imath]a_n,b_n[/imath]. To satisfy the top and bottom boundary conditions, you should choose [imath]a_n,b_n[/imath] such that
[imath]u(x,0)=g(x)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n \sin(n \pi x/a)[/imath]
and
[imath]u(x,b)=f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty (a_n \cosh(n \pi b/a) + b_n \sinh(n \pi b/a)) \sin(n \pi x/a).[/imath]
Now for each [imath]n[/imath] you get two linear equations in the two unknowns [imath]a_n,b_n[/imath], via the Fourier coefficients of [imath]f[/imath] and [imath]g[/imath].

There are numbers [imath]d_n,e_n[/imath] such that [imath]g(x)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty d_n \sin(n \pi x/a)[/imath] and [imath]f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty e_n \sin(n \pi x/a)[/imath]. These numbers should be equal to the coefficients appearing in these two representations of [imath]g[/imath] and [imath]f[/imath]. So in particular [imath]a_n=d_n[/imath], but now [imath]b_n[/imath] will wind up being defined by a slightly complicated expression involving both [imath]d_n[/imath] and [imath]e_n[/imath].
