Transformation of Parameter

Let [imath]S=\mathbb{S}^2[/imath] and let [imath]F_1(x_1,x_2)=\left(x_1,x_2,\sqrt{1-(x_1^2+x_2^2)}\right)[/imath] and [imath]F_2(x_1,x_3)=\left(x_1,\sqrt{1-x_1^2-x_3^2},x_3\right)[/imath] two parametrizations. Furthermore, [imath](u_1,u_2)\in U_1=\{u:\|u\|<1\}[/imath] and [imath]H=F_2^{-1}\circ F_1[/imath]. Find explicitly [imath]H[/imath] and show that [imath]H[/imath] is differentiable in [imath]U_1[/imath].

You need to compute the inverse of [imath]F_2[/imath]. First write,
[imath]F_2(x_1,x_3) = \left(x_1, \sqrt{1-x_1^2-x_3^2},x_3\right) = \begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \\ w \end{pmatrix}[/imath]
So we need [imath]g(u,v,w) = (x_1,x_3)[/imath]. Recalling that [imath](u,v,w) \in \textbf{Im}(F_2)[/imath], take [imath]g(u,v,w) = (u,w)[/imath]. I will show that [imath]g[/imath] is the right-inverse of [imath]F_2[/imath], and I'll leave you to show the left inverse portion. Here we have that,
\begin{align*}(F_2 \circ g)(u,v,w) &= F_2(g(u,v,w)) \\&= F_2(u,v) \\&= F_2(x_1,x_3) \\&= (u,v,w) \end{align*}
Now that we have [imath]F_2^{-1}:=g[/imath], we can compute [imath]H[/imath]. I will leave you to show that [imath]H[/imath] is differentiable over the set [imath]U_1[/imath].
\begin{align*} H(x_1,x_2) &= (F_2^{-1} \circ F_1)(x_1,x_2) \\ & = F_2^{-1}\left(x_1, x_2, \sqrt{1-(x_1^2+x_2^2)}\right) \\ &= \left(x_1, \sqrt{1-(x_1^2+x_2^2)}\right) \end{align*}
