Given a set of all continuous functions from the interval [imath][a,b][/imath] to the real numbers [imath]\mathbb{R}[/imath], i.e. [imath]C([a,b],\mathbb{R}).[/imath]
Let [imath]G :C([a,b],\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow \mathbb{R} , f \mapsto \max_{s \in [a,b]}(f(s))[/imath]
and
Let [imath] \lVert \cdot \rVert_{\infty} :C([a,b],\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, f \mapsto \lVert f \rVert_{\infty}:= \max_{s \in [a,b]}(|f(s)|)[/imath]
Show that [imath]G[/imath] is continuous with respect to the norm [imath] \lVert \cdot \rVert_{\infty} [/imath].
My question hereto is: how should I understand the last sentence, specifically the part "with respect to"? Is it possible to apply the norm [imath] \lVert \cdot \rVert_{\infty} [/imath] to [imath]G[/imath], and if so, what would the mapping look like?
Both [imath]G[/imath] and [imath] \lVert \cdot \rVert_{\infty} [/imath] are maps from [imath]C([a,b],\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}[/imath], hence I am unsure of how to use [imath]G[/imath] and [imath] \lVert \cdot \rVert_{\infty} [/imath] together. To do it as "[imath] \lVert G\rVert_{\infty} [/imath]" is not intuitive to me.

We are to take the distance between two functions [imath]f,g[/imath] to be [imath]\|f-g\|_\infty.[/imath] That is implied by the mention of continuity "with respect to" that norm.
The distance between two real numbers [imath]a,b[/imath] is [imath]|a-b|.[/imath]
"Continuity" of [imath]G[/imath] means:
\begin{align}
& \text{For every member } f \text{ of the domain } C([a,b],\mathbb{R}), \\[5pt]
& \text{for every number } \varepsilon>0 \text{ (no matter how small)}, \\[5pt]
& \text{there is some number } \delta>0 \text{ so small that whenever} \\[5pt]
& g \text{ is any member of the domain } C([a,b], \mathbb R) \text{ for which} \\[5pt]
& \text{the distance between } f \text{ and } g \text{ is less than } \delta, \\[5pt]
& \text{the distance between } G(f) \text{ and } G(g) \text{ is less than } \varepsilon.
\end{align}
More tersely,
\begin{align}
\forall f\in C([a,b],\mathbb R)\ \forall \varepsilon > 0\ \exists\delta > 0 & \ \forall g\in C([a,b],\mathbb R)\\[10pt]
& \Big( \text{if } \underbrace{\|f-g\|_\infty} < \delta \text{ then } \underbrace{|G(f) - G(g)|}<\varepsilon \Big).
\end{align}
The two [imath]\underbrace{\text{underbraces}}[/imath] correspond to the metric on the domain and the metric on the codomain.
To prove this, you will of course need the definition of [imath]G[/imath]. And some basic inequalities.

I have switched [imath]\delta[/imath] with [imath]\varepsilon[/imath] to the correct form.

In a domain of [imath]G[/imath] we have the [imath]\|\cdot\|_{\infty}[/imath] norm, so continuity wrt. this norm means that if [imath]\|f_n-f\|_{\infty}\to 0[/imath] (as [imath]n\to\infty[/imath]) then [imath]|G(f_n)-G(f)|\to 0[/imath]. Unfortunately our functional [imath]G[/imath] is not linear because the maximum property loses after multiplying by negative scalars. So, it is not enough to check continuity at [imath]0[/imath]-function.
