If I understand well, [imath]A_{\mathcal B}[/imath] is the matrix of [imath]F[/imath] in the basis of eigenvectors. So it should be the diagonal matrix with (in this order) [imath]1/2, 0,0[/imath] on the diagonal.

Let's try a shortcut. The equation of the kernel tells you that [imath]A \begin{pmatrix} x \\ x+2z \\ z \end{pmatrix} = 0[/imath]. The eigenvalue equation tells you that [imath]A \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \dfrac 1 2 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}[/imath].
Taking [imath]x = z = 1[/imath] gives you [imath]0 = A \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = A \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + A \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} = \dfrac 1 2 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + 3 A e_2[/imath], whence [imath]A e_2 = - \dfrac 1 6 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = - \dfrac 1 6 e_1 - \dfrac 1 6 e_3[/imath].
Taking now [imath]x=0[/imath] and [imath]z=1[/imath] gives [imath]0 = A \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = 2 A e_2 + A e_3 = - \dfrac 1 3 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + A e_3[/imath], whence [imath]A e_3 = \dfrac 1 3 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \dfrac 1 3 e_1 + \dfrac 1 3 e_3[/imath].
Taking [imath]x=1[/imath] and [imath]z=0[/imath] gives [imath]0 = A \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} = A e_1 + A e_2[/imath], whence [imath]A e_1 = \dfrac 1 6 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \dfrac 1 6 e_1 + \dfrac 1 6 e_3[/imath].
It follows that [imath]A = \begin{pmatrix} \dfrac 1 6 & - \dfrac 1 6 & \dfrac 1 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \dfrac 1 6 & - \dfrac 1 6 & \dfrac 1 3 \end{pmatrix} = \dfrac 1 6 \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}[/imath].
