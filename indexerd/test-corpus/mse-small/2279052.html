However, [imath]\Bbb Q[/imath] is closed in [imath]\Bbb Q[/imath].
When analysing the continuity of a function [imath]g:A\to B[/imath], then you need to analyse, for any open / closed [imath]U\subseteq B[/imath], whether [imath]f^{-1}(U)[/imath] is open / closed in [imath]A[/imath]. It doesn't matter whether [imath]A[/imath] is thought of as a subspace of some larger space; in this context that larger space does not exist for our intents and purposes.
So in our case, when we have a function [imath]f:\Bbb Q\to \Bbb R[/imath], you must forget that [imath]\Bbb Q[/imath] is a part of [imath]\Bbb R[/imath], and just look at [imath]\Bbb Q[/imath] as a topological space in its own right.
