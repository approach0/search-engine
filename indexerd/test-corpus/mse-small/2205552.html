When homogeneous coordinate ring of an Abelian variety is Gorenstein?

Let [imath]A[/imath] be an abelian variety of dimension at least [imath]2[/imath]. If [imath]A[/imath] is embedded into a projective space by a very ample line bundle [imath]\mathcal{L}[/imath] under which assumptions on [imath]\mathcal{L}[/imath] the homogeneous coordinate ring
[imath]
R = \oplus_{i \geq 0} H^0(A, \mathcal{L}^i)
[/imath]
of [imath]A[/imath] is a graded Gorenstein ring?

This ring is never Cohen-Macaulay and hence can not be Gorenstein.
How one can prove this? Any references?
If it were CM, it is very easy to check that [imath]H^i(L^k)=0[/imath] for all [imath]k[/imath] and [imath]01[/imath] and [imath]H^1(\mathcal{O}_A)\neq 0[/imath], you are done.

The definition of Gorenstein ring is a local one, a ring [imath]R[/imath] is Gorenstein if for every prime ideal [imath]R_p[/imath] is a local Gorenstein ring.
For an abelian variety, you will have a Gorenstein ring which is not arithmetically Gorenstein, i.e. if you look at the global equations they do not determine a Conhen-Macaulay ideal. On the other hand, for every localisation it is Gorenstein (and hence CM).
