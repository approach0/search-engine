Asymptote to graph of solution to [imath]60y' = y[12 - y][/imath]

[imath]60P'(t) = P(t)[12 - P(t)][/imath], and [imath]P(0) = 3[/imath]. What is the horizontal asymptote for the graph of the function [imath]P(t)[/imath]? The answer is "[imath]y = 12[/imath]." The answer is the same if the initial condition is [imath]20[/imath] or [imath]100[/imath]. What is the solution to the differential equation?


Let [imath]y=P(t)[/imath]. Then
[imath]\begin{align}60y'=y(12-y)&\implies\frac{60}{y(12-y)}\frac{dy}{dt}=1\tag1\\
\int\frac5y+\frac{5}{12-y}dy&=\int dt\tag2\\
5\log y-5\log(12-y)&=t+c\tag3\\
\log\frac{12-y}{y}&=-\frac15t+c\tag4\\
12-y&=Aye^{-t/5}\tag5\\
y&=\frac{12}{1+Ae^{-t/5}}\tag6\end{align}[/imath]
As [imath]t\to\infty[/imath], [imath]y\to \frac{12}{1+Ae^{-"\infty"}}=\frac{12}{1+0}=12.[/imath]
Also notice that the initial condition allows us to determine [imath]A[/imath], which has no affect on this asymptote, as you noted, since [imath]Ae^{-t/5}\to0[/imath] as [imath]t\to\infty[/imath] regardless of [imath]A[/imath].

[imath](3)[/imath] - integrate
[imath](4)[/imath] - log rules
[imath](5)[/imath] and [imath](6)[/imath] - rearrange
